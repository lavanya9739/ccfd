Machine Learning

ML algorithms analyze past transaction data to build models that predict the likelihood of fraudulent activities. It identifies patterns in historical transactions, recognizing anomalies that indicate possible fraud. Additionally, ML adapts to new fraud techniques by learning from fresh data, ensuring it evolves with changing fraud tactics.

Ensemble Learning

Ensemble Learning improves prediction accuracy by combining multiple models. It works by aggregating the predictions of various base models, such as decision trees, and leveraging their collective strength to make more accurate predictions. This approach increases robustness and reduces overfitting, making fraud detection more reliable, even with complex data patterns.
